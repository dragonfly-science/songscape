Paua are harvested from multiple small reefs and beds. When these are combined, the total area fished in each region is no bigger than a kiwifruit or apple orchard. Paua Industry Council chief executive officer Jeremy Cooper explains.

“In order to be effective, the scale of our information needs to match the scale of our fishing effort. At the moment we use statistical areas to gauge our fishing effort, but those might not be appropriate for paua. In some cases those areas cover 20km or more of coastline, but only contain a few reefs suitable for paua harvest.”

The paua logger programme started in October 2010. Two different data loggers are deployed in the field, a boat unit collecting catch and diver identity information, and a turtle unit worn by divers, which collects information on the time, location and depth of dives. The data logger units are manufactured by Zebra-Tech in Nelson. Zebra-Tech specialises in the design, development and manufacture of environmental instrumentation and equipment for environmental monitoring and research. Development of the paua loggers is partly funded by the New Zealand Government, through Seafood Innovations.

Data from the loggers are stored in a secure database developed by Dragonfly Science and Catalyst IT in Wellington. Logger files are uploaded into the database via this website. Summaries of the logger data are available to registered users.
